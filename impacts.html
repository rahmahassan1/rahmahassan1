Impacts!

Health

Annually, 1 million people die from water, sanitation and hygiene-related diseases which could be reduced with access to safe water or sanitation. Children are especially susceptible to contracting water-related illnesses like diarrhoea, typhoid, dehydration. These diseases are long-term as they affect the development of the child.

Economy

When you spend all your time looking for water you lose valuable time which leads to billions lost in economic opportunities. Having access to safe clean water would help reduce healthcare expenses and reduce time spent looking for water. Universal access to water would result in $18.5 billion in economic benefits each year from avoided deaths alone!

Education

Children often miss school as they have to collect water for their families. It is estimated that children around the world spend 200 million hours each day collecting water. Menstruation is also a reason why young girls miss school. This sets up a cycle of poverty and inequality as without a proper education, there is little chance of improving your life.
